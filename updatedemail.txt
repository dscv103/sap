Subject: Rework Is Driving Closing Delays — Data Summary and Recommendation

Hi [Leader],

Cycle-time data for booked loans from January 1 through March 16 reveals a significant and addressable opportunity: rework originating in earlier stages is consuming a majority of Closing time in both credit card portfolios.

Note: Rework tracking was implemented in late January, so January figures are partial. The rates below are likely understated.

The closing-adjusted figure is total Closing stage duration minus rework time within Closing. All figures are for booked loans only.

Business Banking–Credit Card (126 booked loans, 68 sent for rework — 54%)
  • Avg Closing: 6.96 days | Closing Adjusted: 3.07 days | Rework: 3.89 days
  • Rework accounts for 56% of all Business Banking Closing time
  • A loan that hits rework averages 7.22 days of rework alone
  • Clean loans (no rework) still average 6.67 days in Closing

The clean loan figure for Business Banking warrants further investigation. Eliminating rework brings the average down to 3.07 days, but loans that never enter rework are still averaging 6.67 days — suggesting rework is not the only driver of extended Closing time for this portfolio. Possible explanations include:

  • Inherent product complexity: BB credit applications typically require entity documentation, beneficial ownership verification, multiple signatories, and more complex closing packages than consumer loans, independent of any rework.
  • Rework undercounting: With tracking implemented in late January, some activity on loans currently classified as clean may represent unrecorded rework, artificially inflating the clean loan average.
  • Multi-party coordination: Business closings often involve attorneys, multiple officers, or third parties that extend turnaround regardless of internal process quality.
  • Queue or staffing dynamics: BB loans may sit in a specialized queue with different capacity constraints than consumer loans.
  • Sub-product mix: Some loan types within BB may have legitimately longer closing requirements and could be pulling the average up. Segmenting by sub-product may isolate this.

This is a separate but related finding that merits its own review.

Consumer–Credit Card (1,034 booked loans, 164 sent for rework — 16%)
  • Avg Closing: 1.34 days | Closing Adjusted: 0.47 days | Rework: 0.87 days
  • Rework accounts for 65% of all Consumer Closing time
  • 16% of loans are consuming 65% of total Closing time
  • A rework loan averages 5.48 days — nearly 10x the 0.56-day average for a clean loan

The Consumer finding reframes the problem: this is not a broad performance issue across all loans. A small share of loans, when they hit rework, consume a disproportionate amount of Closing capacity. Clean Consumer loans close in under a day on average, which confirms that the process works well when applications arrive in good order.

Why this matters beyond cycle time:

  Cost. Rework loans consume dramatically more Closing staff time than clean loans — up to 10x more for Consumer. Reducing rework incidence lowers per-application cost without changes to Closing team capacity or structure.

  Customer experience. Customers who reach Closing expect to be nearly done. A 5- to 7-day rework delay at that stage creates friction at exactly the wrong moment.

  Operational risk. Late corrections under time pressure carry higher exposure to errors, inconsistent handling, and compliance gaps than issues caught and resolved earlier in the workflow.

Recommended next steps:

  1. Identify the upstream defect categories most commonly driving Closing rework in both portfolios.
  2. Investigate why clean Business Banking loans are still averaging 6.67 days in Closing, to determine how much of that is structural versus addressable.
